# Koshino's effective model of Magic-Angle Twisted Bilayer Graphene

This model describes the MATBG low-energy band structure through a list of hopping integrals $t_{ij}$ between maximally localized Wannier orbitals.

These orbitals have a three-peak form at AA spots and are centered at nonequivalent BA (orbital 1) and AB (orbital 2) spots in the emergent moir√© pattern, creating an alternating honeycomb lattice. The orbitals for valley $\xi = +$ are the complex conjugate of $\xi = -$, so the center of orbitals 1 and 2 are switched (?). Due to symmetries under rotation, there orbitals are $p_\xi \equiv p_x + i\xi p_y$. The effective model is then constructed by hopping integrals between $(p_x, p_y)$ orbitals.

Each hopping integral is a complex number, but among the nearest five integrals, only $t_2$ has a non-zero imaginary component, due to "hexagonal warping effect in orbital space".

Based on doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevX.8.031087 model and parameters.
